I'm a writer turned web developer ...

For the first 7 years of my career, I built a successful career in writing and editing. I worked with magazines and websites to do everything from marketing content to editorial production.

The more closely I worked with websites, in particular, the more interested I became in web development. Observation and my own foray into customizing a few WordPress websites left me with two observations:

1 - Rhetorical Goals

The goals of writing and web development are not all that different. In both cases, the constant consideration is the reader or user. How can we reach an individual? How can we add value? How can we guide them into utilizing our end-product the way we intended?

2 - Creative & Precise

Web development is the perfect union of the editor and writer in me. Editing is about precision and technical understanding. From proofing to content editing, it is all about utilizing technical knowledge to make writing shine. At the same time, writing has always been an outlet for my creative side. In web development, I can express both. The technicality of code is obvious, but the unlimited opportunities to use it are an unexpected and delightful outlet for creativity.

Life and a move to South Korea (and back) presented me with the perfect opportunity to make the leap between industries. So, here I am!
